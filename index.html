About

home

Game-Off is a game competition held monthly on Scratch. It lasts for a single week during which contestants create and enter a game coded in the Scratch language. Each contest has a theme to which entrants' games must adhere to.

Rules

info

Below is a list of general rules that all entrants must follow for their entries to be accepted.

All entries must be coded and submitted on Scratch.

All entrants must use the Scratch coding language to create their games. Any games not created using this language are not permitted and will be disqualified without repeal.

Entries may contain no stolen material.

The game you submit must contain assets created exclusively by you with exception to music, which must be properly attributed. That means that all code and graphics featured in the project must be created by your own hand, or that of a team member's.

Entries must be created within the assigned week.

All entrants have exactly one week to create their game, and they may submit only one within this timeframe. If an entrant or team submits more than one project, none will be judged. You may revise your game before the time limit or enter a new game as long as your old entry is unshared.

Teams

Teamwork is allowed, but inter-teamwork is not. Teams may have up to three people, all of whom will share the spoils. Please credit your team members so they may receive the prizes they deserve, given your group wins the competition.

Entry

Please enter your game to a contest by remixing the contest project. Entries that are not remixes will not be judged. If you are having an issue remixing the project, please use the contact information below to notify us of your issue.

Any questions?

If you have any other questions regarding the rules of Game-Off competitions, please contact us. We are willing to answer any questions and hear any complaints you may have.
